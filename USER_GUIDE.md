## Tips for Success

### Before the Accident (Preparation)

- Keep insurance documents in glovebox
- Know your policy number
- Consider dashcam installation
- Take photos of your car's condition regularly

### At the Scene

1. **Safety first** - Move to safe location if possible
2. **Don't admit fault** - Even "sorry" can be used against you
3. **Exchange details** - Name, insurance, registration
4. **Take photos** - Vehicles, damage, scene, road conditions
5. **Get witnesses** - Name and contact details
6. **Note everything** - Time, weather, what happened
7. **Call police** - If injury, dispute, or hit-and-run

### Completing Your Report

1. **Do it soon** - Memory fades; complete within 24 hours
2. **Be thorough** - More detail is better
3. **Be honest** - Don't exaggerate or minimize
4. **Use voice feature** - Often faster and more detailed
5. **Include timeline** - Chronological order helps
6. **Attach everything** - Photos, receipts, medical reports

### After Submission

1. **Download PDF** - Keep multiple copies
2. **Contact insurer** - Within 24 hours
3. **Don't repair yet** - Insurer needs to assess
4. **Keep receipts** - Taxi, hire car, medical expenses
5. **Follow medical advice** - Document all appointments
6. **Don't discuss online** - Social media can harm claims

## Glossary

**GDPR** - General Data Protection Regulation (UK data privacy law)

**Third Party** - The other driver in the accident

**Excess** - Amount you pay toward claim before insurance covers rest

**No Claims Bonus** - Discount for claim-free years

**Liability** - Legal responsibility for the accident

**RTA** - Road Traffic Accident

**what3words** - Location system using three words (e.g., table.lamp.border)

**Dashcam** - Dashboard-mounted camera recording driving

**Whiplash** - Neck injury from sudden movement (common in rear-end collisions)

**Write-off** - Vehicle damage too expensive to repair
